How to calculate percentages

A percentage is a number expressed as a fraction of 100. For example, 25% means 25 out of every 100.

To find X% of Y: multiply Y by X and divide by 100. For example, 25% of 200 = (200 × 25) ÷ 100 = 50.

Percentage change is calculated as: ((new value − old value) ÷ old value) × 100. A positive result means an increase, a negative result means a decrease.
