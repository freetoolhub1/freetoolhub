What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by your height in metres squared. Formula: BMI = weight (kg) divided by height squared (m2). It provides a quick screening tool to categorise individuals as underweight, healthy, overweight, or obese.

BMI Categories Explained

  • Under 18.5 — Underweight: May indicate nutritional deficiencies
  • 18.5 to 24.9 — Healthy weight: Lowest risk of weight-related health issues
  • 25 to 29.9 — Overweight: Increased risk of cardiovascular disease and diabetes
  • 30 and above — Obese: Significantly elevated risk of serious health conditions

How to Calculate Your BMI

Limitations of BMI

  • Does not distinguish between fat and muscle mass
  • Does not account for fat distribution (abdominal fat is more dangerous)
  • May overestimate obesity in muscular athletes
  • Always consult a healthcare professional for a complete assessment
Frequently Asked Questions
What is a healthy BMI?+
18.5 to 24.9 is considered healthy. Under 18.5 is underweight. 25-29.9 is overweight. 30 and above is obese.
Is BMI accurate for everyone?+
BMI is a general guide. It does not account for muscle mass, bone density, age, or ethnicity. Athletes often have high BMI despite being healthy.
Does BMI differ for men and women?+
The standard BMI scale is the same for both. However women naturally carry more body fat at the same BMI.
What should I do if my BMI is unhealthy?+
Consult a healthcare professional who can assess your full health picture and provide personalised advice.
Can I use this for children?+
Standard BMI charts are for adults 20+. Children should use age-specific charts. Consult a paediatrician for accurate assessment.
