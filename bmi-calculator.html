What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It is calculated by dividing weight in kilograms by height in metres squared (kg/m²).

A BMI under 18.5 is considered underweight, 18.5–24.9 is healthy, 25–29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is considered obese.

Note: BMI is a general guide and does not account for muscle mass, age, or other factors. Consult a doctor for personalised health advice.
