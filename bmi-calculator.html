What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by your height in metres squared. Formula: BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)2. It provides a quick screening tool to categorise weight status.

BMI Categories Explained

  • Under 18.5 — Underweight: May indicate nutritional deficiencies
  • 18.5 to 24.9 — Healthy weight: Lowest risk of weight-related health issues
  • 25 to 29.9 — Overweight: Increased risk of cardiovascular disease and diabetes
  • 30 and above — Obese: Significantly elevated health risks

How to Calculate Your BMI

Limitations of BMI

  • Does not distinguish between fat and muscle mass
  • Does not account for fat distribution
  • May overestimate obesity in muscular athletes
  • Always consult a healthcare professional for a complete assessment
Frequently Asked Questions
What is a healthy BMI?+
18.5 to 24.9 is considered healthy. Under 18.5 is underweight. 25-29.9 is overweight. 30 and above is obese.
Is BMI accurate for everyone?+
BMI is a general guide and does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or ethnicity. Athletes often have high BMI despite being healthy.
Does BMI differ for men and women?+
The standard scale is the same, but women naturally carry slightly more body fat at the same BMI value.
What should I do if my BMI is unhealthy?+
Consult a healthcare professional for a complete assessment and personalised advice.
Can I use this for children?+
Standard BMI is for adults 20+. Children should use age-specific BMI charts. Consult a paediatrician.
