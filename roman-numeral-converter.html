Roman Numeral Rules

  • Symbols are generally written from largest to smallest, left to right
  • When a smaller symbol precedes a larger one, subtract it: IV = 4, IX = 9, XL = 40, XC = 90, CD = 400, CM = 900
  • You can repeat a symbol up to 3 times: III = 3, XXX = 30, CCC = 300, MMM = 3000
  • V, L, and D are never repeated

Common Roman Numerals

  • 2026 = MMXXVI | 2000 = MM | 1999 = MCMXCIX
  • 100 = C | 500 = D | 1000 = M
  • 50 = L | 40 = XL | 90 = XC
Frequently Asked Questions
What is the maximum number?+
Standard Roman numerals go up to 3999 (MMMCMXCIX). Numbers above this are not standard.
Are Roman numerals still used today?+
Yes. Clock faces, book chapters, movie sequels, Super Bowl numbers, and year copyrights often use Roman numerals.
What is the Roman numeral for 2026?+
2026 = MMXXVI (MM=2000, XX=20, VI=6).
Can I convert any Roman numeral?+
Yes. Enter any valid Roman numeral (using I, V, X, L, C, D, M) to convert it to a number.
