Why Does Copied Text Have Random Line Breaks?

When you copy text from a PDF, Word document, or email, the line breaks from the original layout are preserved. This creates broken sentences when you paste into a new document. The Smart mode fixes this in one click while keeping your paragraph spacing.

When to Use Each Mode

  • Smart mode — best for PDF text. Removes mid-sentence breaks but keeps paragraph spacing.
  • Remove all — converts entire text to one continuous paragraph.
  • Remove extra spaces — cleans up multiple consecutive spaces and excessive blank lines.
  • Single to double — adds extra spacing between every line.
Frequently Asked Questions
What does 'Smart (keep paragraphs)' do?+
It removes single line breaks like those from PDF copy-paste, but keeps double line breaks that separate paragraphs. This is the most useful mode for most situations.
Why does copied PDF text have broken lines?+
PDFs have fixed line widths. When you copy text, the line endings are preserved as newlines even though they were just visual wrapping.
Does 'Remove all breaks' remove paragraph spacing too?+
Yes — it converts the entire text into one continuous paragraph. Use Smart mode if you want to keep paragraph spacing.
Is there a character limit?+
No limit. Paste any length of text.
