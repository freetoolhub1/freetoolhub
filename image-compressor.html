What is an Image Compressor?

An image compressor reduces the file size of an image without noticeably affecting its visual quality. Our free tool works entirely in your browser — nothing uploaded to any server.

Why Compress Images for SEO?

Google's Core Web Vitals measure page loading speed. Compressed images help you:

  • Improve Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) score
  • Reduce page weight by 50-80%
  • Rank higher due to better PageSpeed scores
  • Reduce mobile data usage for visitors

Quality Settings Guide

90-100% — Minimal compression

Very little file size reduction. Use for professional printing or when absolute quality is required.

70-85% — Recommended for web

The sweet spot. File size drops 50-75% with virtually no visible quality loss.

50-70% — High compression

Noticeable reduction. Good for thumbnails or low-bandwidth situations.

How to Compress an Image

  1. Click Choose Image or drag your JPG or PNG into the upload area
  2. Use the quality slider (75% recommended)
  3. See live preview comparing original vs compressed size
  4. Click Compress and Download to save your smaller image
Frequently Asked Questions
Does compression reduce quality?+
Our tool lets you control quality from 1-100%. At 75-85% most users cannot see any difference while file size drops by 50-80%.
What formats are supported?+
JPG and PNG input. Output is saved as compressed JPG.
Is my image uploaded to a server?+
No. Everything happens in your browser. Your image never leaves your device.
Why should I compress images for websites?+
Smaller images load faster, improving Google PageSpeed scores, Core Web Vitals, and user experience. Google ranks fast sites higher.
What quality setting is recommended?+
75% gives the best balance of file size reduction and visual quality for most use cases.
