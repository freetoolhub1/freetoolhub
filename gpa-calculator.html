GPA Scale Explained

  • 4.0 — A: Excellent performance. Most scholarships and honours programmes require 3.5+
  • 3.0 — B: Good performance. Typical requirement for graduate school admission
  • 2.0 — C: Satisfactory. Minimum GPA to remain in good academic standing at most colleges
  • 1.0 — D: Poor performance. Many programmes require a retake
  • 0.0 — F: Fail. No credit earned
Frequently Asked Questions
What is GPA?+
Grade Point Average — a number (usually 0-4.0) that represents your average academic performance across all courses.
Does each course affect GPA equally?+
No. Higher-credit courses have more weight. A 4-credit course affects your GPA twice as much as a 2-credit course.
What is a good GPA?+
3.5+ is considered very good and qualifies for most academic honours. 3.0+ is good. Below 2.0 is often academic probation.
What is the difference between GPA and CGPA?+
GPA is for a single term. CGPA (Cumulative GPA) covers all semesters combined and is what most employers and graduate schools look at.
