BEST FREE QR CODE GENERATOR 2026 — CREATE IN SECONDS

📅 April 25, 2026 ⏱ 5 min read 📝 FreeToolHub

QR codes are everywhere in 2026. Restaurant menus, business cards, product packaging, event tickets, and social media profiles all use them. Creating a professional QR code used to require expensive software. Now you can do it in seconds, completely free, with no app to download or account to create.

What Can a QR Code Contain?

  • Website URLs — the most common use. Link to any webpage instantly
  • Contact information (vCard) — tap to save contact details directly to phone
  • Wi-Fi credentials — guests connect without typing a password
  • Social media profiles — link directly to Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, or YouTube
  • Plain text or addresses — show any information or open in Maps
  • Pre-filled emails — tap to open a compose window with your email pre-loaded

QR Code Size Guide — What Size for What Purpose?

  • 200×200px: Digital use only — social media posts and email signatures
  • 300×300px: Business cards (minimum recommended for reliable scanning)
  • 400×400px: Flyers, menus, and A5 materials
  • 600×600px: Posters, banners, and large format print materials

Error Correction — Why It Matters for Print

Error correction allows a QR code to scan correctly even if part of it is damaged, obscured, or worn. There are four levels — Low (7%), Medium (15%), Quartile (25%), and High (30%). For anything printed on physical materials, always use High error correction. This allows up to 30% of the QR code to be damaged and it will still scan correctly.

How to Create a Custom QR Code Free

QR Code Best Practices — Do Not Skip These

  • Always test before printing — scan on both iPhone and Android and test the destination URL
  • Dark colours on light backgrounds only — inverted light-on-dark QR codes often fail to scan
  • Minimum 2cm × 2cm on printed materials — smaller QR codes frequently fail on standard phone cameras
  • Include a call to action — add "Scan to visit our menu" or "Scan for discount" near the code
  • Check the link regularly — static QR codes break permanently if you change the destination URL
